PROJECT IDEA

Overview

For my IT Project idea, I have decided to write about an idea I had a few years ago when I was a music teacher. This idea goes on the lines of automated online tuition for guitar where the each part has to be completed to go onto the next lesson. I was thinking that at the end of each lesson the student would actually have to play the music back of what they learnt in the lesson to progress to the next one. If they fail to playback the music correctly it would mean they would have to practice until they get it right.

Motivation

When I was a teacher I found that I could always tell which students had learnt from outside sources such as youtube as there would always be errors in their technique and they would not really be playing the instrument correctly. Most times they would lose interest and give up on the instrument rather than persevere and master what they want to get out of it. Ultimately we would definitely want students not to feel disheartened and want them to really enjoy playing. Learning music online can have its advantages such as students for the most time have to learn by ear (Marianne Stenger, 2013) and can pick tonal qualities up easier than students who have learnt from instruction.

Description

What I want to design is an online guitar tuition application that will be used to teach students how to play and can be viewed on mobile devices, desktops, and tablets. In recent times with a lot of schools facing budget cuts, their music programmes are normally the first thing to be cut (Marianne Stenger, 2013). Therefore I believe there is a market for online guitar tuition. This could also include connecting students with teachers so they could get feedback with what they are doing. This is a reason to why I believe students that only rely on youtube to learn, do not really learn how to play their instrument properly and have various flaws in their techniques.

Lessons would have to start with a prompt to tune the instrument so it would be good to have a tuner as an in-built feature. We need to tune the instrument so that the examples can be played along with, without sounding out of tune. The lessons would ideally be no longer then what could be covered in 30 minutes. I would like the layout of the page in the app to have written instructions and examples to play along with. I also think it would be beneficial to also include verbal instructional videos to back up what is written because all students pick up information in different ways as is their learning style (teach.com, 2020). A lot of lessons will also be repeating what the student learnt in the previous lessons as repetition reinforces principles (Sara Mullett, 2018).

One possible issue that I think may arise from this project is the use of copyrighted material. The rights of the owners of the copyrighted material include both performing the track in public and also communicating the material to members of the public (Music Rights Australia n.d.). A lot of classical music that was released earlier than the 20th century is considered “free Domain” which means the material is not copyrighted and can be freely shared. Often if the music is made before 1923, it is most likely not covered by copyright (Tim Berens, n.d.). Therefore I can take from this is that most musical exercises need to be based off music that is over 100 years old (i.e. ‘Ode to Joy’ by Beethoven) or written as an original.

The lessons can have various elements to it for example I would start with technique and a few songs to play to get the student hooked and motivated and then go on to things like theory and music fundamentals such as simple rhythms, learning notes, and where to find them, chords and intervals, modes, arpeggios and scales. In all there are a lot of options to fit into a musical class and it is important to have variety and flexibility in planning lessons if a child only learns theory before even playing their instrument they are likely to get bored and will give up.

Tools and Technologies

So we know what we want to develop; now how do we go and make it happen? I believe an app such as MIT App Inventor can help in creating the core of the app. MIT App Inventor was invented at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and was spearheaded by Professor Hal Abelson (MIT App Inventor n.d.). I was also thinking the website could be HTML/CSS with video streamed into it. The video might need to be converted to Flash Video File (*.flv) and embedded into the code. Obviously there will need to be hardware such as musical instruments, an audio interface such as a Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 to get the music signal into the computer, and a program such as a DAW (Digital Audio Workstation, i.e. Logic Pro X, Audacity, Cubase, Reaper) and record the musical content.

Skills Required

For this project, we will need various types of skills. The obvious one would be to know Music theory and be able to demonstrate on a musical instrument. There will be presentation skills, knowing how to hold an audience and relate information to students so they can learn. On the computing side there is app writing skills that could include programming (CSS and HTML5), also languages such as C+ and Java could help write the application (Kevin Toth, 2017). Also I believe I will have to learn video editing skills such as using video editing software (such as) So that I can record the sessions.

Outcome

The outcome of the project is to get potential students to learn and enjoy playing an instrument. I believe there is a market for people who want to learn how to play guitar and to play it well. There has been a spike in people trying to learn a new instrument since the global pandemic of Covid 19 (TMZ, 2020). I would like to get students to have great joy in knowing how to play with correct technique. Also, I would like to be able to develop the skills to put a product on the market like this.
